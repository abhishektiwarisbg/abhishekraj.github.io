IEEE, 2022

Proceedings of 2nd ASIANCON 2022

Accepted for publication

In recent years, machine learning-based identification of Alzheimer's disease (AD) from the use of brain images such as MRI has been a hot topic. Deep learning's recent triumph in object recognition has stimulated related research. Such algorithms, however, have some disadvantages, including the requirement for a large number of training pictures and rigorous deep network design optimization. To address these issues, we employ CNN and ANN in this study. Weights are pre-trained on huge natural picture benchmark datasets in state-of-the-art designs like EfficientNetV2, and only a few numbers of MRI images are used to retrain the comprehensive layers in state-of-the-art architectures like EfficientNetV2. Our dataset contains 4 characteristics, as well as 5121 training shots and 1279 testing photos. The data was initially divided into three sets: training, validation, and test, with just the training, validation sets being used to select models. To minimize overfitting, the tests were abandoned undisturbed until the collaborative process was completed. The various techniques performed well when applied to when using datasets with alternative criteria for inclusion or demographic features, this is not the case.

AIP Conference Proceedings (SCOPUS), 2022

Proceedings of International Conference on Advancements in Computation & Computer Technologies (ICACCT-2021)

Accepted for publication

The process of separating tumors from normal brain tissues is known as brain tumor separation. Brain tumor segmentation is a challenging job in artificial intelligence and a crucial topic for study in medical technology. Biomedical imaging solutions based on computer vision are gaining pace, and e-healthcare systems can help in a range of medical domains. They provide radiologists with identification information so that they can better treat patients. Medical imaging techniques and procedures that have a substantial impact on patient diagnosis and treatment include X-rays, Ultrasound, Magnetic Tone Imaging, and Computed Tomography. In this research, we describe a UNet-based approach for constructing segmentations from MRI data. The model will be able to train using the provided data, which includes MRI scans and segmentation pictures. After that, the model can distinguish between the segmentation portions from the scan images that have been presented to it. We employ the cutting-edge UNet architecture to implement it. A convolutional neural network (CNN) was built to provide more precise segmentations. To achieve a minimum accuracy of 99.5 percent, we train our model for at least 40 epochs using training and validation generations.
